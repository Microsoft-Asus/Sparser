BEIRUT , October 27 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Jordan 's Queen Noor arrived here Wednesday to start her first official visit to Lebanon on a global anti-landmine mission .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">Noor , the widow of Jordan 's late King Hussein , held talks with Lebanese President Emile Lahoud on bilateral ties which have soured due to Amman 's signing of a peace treaty with Israel .</ENAMEX>
Lebanon is officially at war with Israel , which set up a self-declared security zone in south Lebanon to allegedly protect its north from Lebanese guerrillas ' cross-border attacks .
Relations between the two countries began to improve after Lahoud and Jordan 's new King Abdullah exchanged visits in May and last month respectively .
During her two-day visit , the queen will witness the defusing of land mines in Lebanon 's mountainous area Souk Gharb , one of the bloodiest fronts in the country 's 1975-1990 civil war .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">The American -born Noor , who is visiting Lebanon as her capacity as patron of the Washington -based Landmine Survivors Network , took over the post after the death of Diana , British Princess of Wales</ENAMEX> .
She will also accompany Lebanese First Lady Andre Lahoud at the inauguration of a fair at the American of University of Beirut .
