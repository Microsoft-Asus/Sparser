TUNIS , August 17 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- The leaders of Libya , Burkina Faso , Chad , Mali and Niger have called for redoubling efforts to find solutions to some African issues , including development and stability .
The leaders issued the call in their joint statement which was issued at the close of their summit meeting Saturday night in the Libyan capital of Tripoli .
The statement , which was published by Libya 's news agency JANA , underlined the necessity of enhancing the rapprochement among African nations as a step toward achieving African unity .
The statement said that leaders of the five African states commissioned their foreign ministers to hold a meeting in Tripoli next month to consider this issue .
About Libya 's dispute with some Western powers , the statement said that leaders of the five African states expressed their anxiety about the continuation of the U.N. sanctions imposed on Libya , which have negative effects on Libya and the economies of the five African states .
The leaders of the five states also made an urgent appeal to the international community to find a fair and ultimate solution to Libya 's dispute with both the United States and Britain .
They called on U.N. Secretary - General Kofi Annan to send a fact-finding commission to Libya to see how U.N. sanctions are affecting the Libyan people .
Libya has been under U.N. sanctions since 1992 for refusing to turn over two Libyans charged in the 1988 bombing of a Pan Am jet over Lockerbie , Scotland which killed 270 people .
The five-nation African summit opened in Tripoli Friday .
