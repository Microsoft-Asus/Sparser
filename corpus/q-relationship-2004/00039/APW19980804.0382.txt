Japan 's new foreign minister plans to visit Moscow in September , a news report said Tuesday .
Masahiko Kamura said he planned to make the trip during a phone conversation with Russian Foreign Minister Yevgeny Primakov , the ITAR - Tass news agency said .
Kamura reiterated that Tokyo 's policy towards Russia would not change despite a recent Japanese government shakeup .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">He said Japan and Russia would work together in line with two summits held in the last year between President Boris Yeltsin and former Japanese Prime Minister Ryutaro Hashimoto .
Despite disagreement over the Kuril Islands , which Russia has controlled since the end of World War II , relations between the two countries have improved in recent years .</ENAMEX>
