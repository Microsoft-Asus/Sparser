CANBERRA , September 18 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Australians who lived in Britain during the outbreak of mad cow 's disease are to be prevented from donating blood .
Radio ABC reported that the Blood Donation Referral committee in the country held a phone conference Monday morning to discuss the latest evidence from a Scottish scientific team which says there is now preliminary evidence that mad cow 's disease can be spread through blood transfusions .
ABC said all state officials have agreed to prevent those lived in Britain for more than six months between 1980 and 1996 from donating blood .
The final decision is to be made by ministers .
The United States , Austria and New Zealand are reported to put the ban in place .
