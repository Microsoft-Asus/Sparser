ISLAMABAD , March 7 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- <ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">Afghanistan 's ruling Taliban militia Tuesday burned tons of narcotics to show their commitment to curbing rampant drug trafficking in the country , Afghan sources said .</ENAMEX>
Taliban soldiers set fire to 355 kilograms of heroin and 2,000 kilograms of hashish in the militia 's headquarters of Kandahar in southern Afghanistan .
All of the burned narcotics were confiscated by the militia in recent months , said the sources .
Tuesday 's drug burning was the second in the last one month.In February , the Taliban burned 3,750 kilograms of hashish and 50 kilograms of heroin , also in Kandahar .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">Afghanistan is the world 's largest producer of opium -- the raw material used to make heroin .
Last year 4,600 tons of opium was produced in the country .
The Taliban , who rule over 80 percent of Afghanistan , including the capital Kabul , say they are trying to curb poppy cultivation , but poverty and lack of funds are the main obstacles .</ENAMEX>
