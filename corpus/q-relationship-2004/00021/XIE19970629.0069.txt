<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">The AL and the OAU has proposed that the two Libyans , suspected of involvement in the bombing of a Pan AM airliner over Lockerbie , Scotland , in 1988 , which killed 270 people , be tried by a justice from Scotland in a third country other than in Britain and the United States</ENAMEX> .
The Egyptian MENA news agency quoted the AL cheif as saying that he had asked U.N. Secretary - General Kofi Annan to persuade the countries concerned with the Lockerbie crisis to accept the proposals which he described as `` fair '' solutions to ending the dispute .
The U.N. imposed sanctions against Tripoli for its refusal to hand over the two Libyans to Britain and the United States .
Libyan leader Mummar Gaddafi has suggested the two be tried anywhere else than in the U.S. and Britain .
`` Libya will not hand over the suspects -LRB- to Britain or the U.S. -RRB- for fear that they will not stand a fair trial there , but it is ready to hand over them to a neutral court , '' Meguid said .
He pledged strenuous Arab efforts to settle the crisis in order to ease the suffering of the Libyan people as a result of the five-year -old embargo .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">Both the OAU and Al have been calling for a prompt lift of international sanctions against Libya over the Lockerbie crisis and aired support for Tripoli 's overtures to settle the dispute</ENAMEX> .
The Libya - West crisis was discussed for the first time early this month at the Harare OAU summit which called for lifting the sanctions and taking temporary measures to help ease the sufferings of the Libyan people .
Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak , during his two-day visit to the Libyan coastal city of Tobruk on June 12-13 , also denounced the U.N. -imposed ban on Libya and urged an immediate removal of it .
