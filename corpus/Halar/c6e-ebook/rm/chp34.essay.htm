Evolution Connection

Provide one characteristic that qualifies humans for membership in each of the following taxa: domain Eukarya, kingdom Animalia, clade Deuterostomia, phylum Chordata, subphylum Vertebrata, clade Gnathostomata, clade Amniota, class Mammalia, order Primates.

The Process of Science

Scientific inquiry often proceeds by trying to make sense of an interesting observation. One such observation concerns patterns of genetic vs. morphological divergence in some vertebrate groups. Amphibian species, for example, show great similarity in morphology, yet are far more genetically divergent than the morphologically diverse birds. A related pattern is evident in chimpanzee-human comparisons: These two species are quite divergent morphologically, yet are nearly identical genetically. Propose one or more hypotheses that might explain these puzzling patterns.

Science, Technology, and Society
