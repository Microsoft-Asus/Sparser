ANKARA , November 19 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Turkey today accused the Patriotic Union of Kurdistan -LRB- PUK -RRB- , a major party in northern Iraq , of creating fresh tensions in the region , according to the Anatolia News Agency .
Turkish Foreign Ministry spokesman Necati Utkan said at a news briefing today that it was the PUK that provoked the recent clashes with its rival Kurdistan Democratic Party -LRB- KDP -RRB- .
The pro- Iranian PUK , and KDP , allegedly backed by Turkey and Iraq , have engaged in continuous battles for the control of northern Iraq since President Saddam Hussein was rendered ineffective in the area by the defeat in the 1990 Gulf War .
Their conflicts intensified since mid- October , causing grave concern in Turkey , the United States and Britain , the three mediators of the Ankara process designed to settle the northern Iraq issue .
Turkey 's Foreign Ministry also warned the PUK office in Ankara against its cooperation with the Kurdish Workers ' Party -LRB- PKK -RRB- , Utkan said .
