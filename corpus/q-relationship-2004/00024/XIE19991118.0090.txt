ANKARA , November 18 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Greek Prime Minister Costas Simitis said in Istanbul on Thursday that Greece supported Turkey 's European Union -LRB- EU -RRB- perspective .
Following a meeting with his Turkish counterpart Bulent Ecevit , Simitis said Greek government wanted all the European governments to be included in the EU , the semi-official Anatolia news agency reported .
Greece , Turkey 's neighboring rival , has long been accused of attempting to block the Euro - Asian country 's entrance into the club of European nations .
Simitis , who came to Istanbul for the Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe Summit on November 18-19 , said that the meeting between him and Ecevit had focused on the Cyprus issue , the Aegean dispute and regional problems within the scope of the international law .
A lasting peace in this region should be instituted between Turkey and Greece , said Simitis .
He also stressed the importance of cooperation between Turkey and Greece against natural disaster , hoping that the two countries will continue to make cooperation in coming days .
The Greek prime minister pointed out that he had observed Turkish people 's willingness to further improving cooperation with Greece during his brief visit to Turkey .
Meanwhile , Ecevit said that he held a positive and productive meeting with Simitis , adding that Turkish and Greek people 's voluntary solidarity had accelerated the dialog progress between the two countries .
He said that `` we should give priority to the relations between Turkey and Greece . ''
Ecevit also said that he had an opportunity to thank Simitis at the meeting for Greece 's contributions to heal the wounds of the quake disasters in Turkey .
Turkey and Greece , both NATO members , have been at odds for decades over issues of territorial waters , air space limits and the delimitation of the continental shelf in the Aegean Sea and the Cyprus issue .
