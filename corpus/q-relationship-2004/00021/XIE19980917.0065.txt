CAIRO , September 16 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Syrian Foreign Minister Farouq al- Sharaa Wednesday called for lifting the U.N. sanctions against Libya and Iraq , the Middle East News Agency reported .
Sharaa , addressing the inaugural session of the Arab League Foreign Ministerial Council here Wednesday , underlined the necessity of ending the Libya - West crisis over the 1988 Lockerbie crisis .
He expressed his country 's backing of the resolutions adopted by the Organization of African Unity -LRB- OAU -RRB- , the Organization of Islamic Conference -LRB- OIC -RRB- and the Non - Aligned Movement -LRB- NAM -RRB- on ending the crisis .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">The OAU in its summit meeting in June adopted a resolution to defy the sanctions starting from this month if the U.S. and Britain fail to make compromise</ENAMEX> .
Sharaa voiced support for the Libyan requests for guarantees that ensure a fair trial of the two Libyan suspects accused of blowing up an American plane in 1988 over Lockerbie , Scotland , which killed 270 people .
Libya 's failure to extradite them incurred the U.N. air , arms and diplomatic embargo on the country in 1992 .
Sharaa also called for lifting the international embargo imposed against Iraq `` according to a specific schedule . ''
Meanwhile , he urged Baghdad to comply with the relevant U.N. Security Council resolutions .
The U.N. imposed sanctions against Iraq in 1990 after its invasion of neighboring Kuwait , which can be removed only after Iraq fully complies with U.N. resolutions and destroys its weapons of mass destruction .
