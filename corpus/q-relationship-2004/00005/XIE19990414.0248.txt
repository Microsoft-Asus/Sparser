COLOMBO , April 14 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Sri Lankan President Chandrika Kumaratunga Wednesday appealed for the dawn of peace and unity in her ethnic conflict-torn country which is celebrating the Sinhala and Tamil New Year .
The majority Sinhalese and the minority Tamils celebrate their common New Year on Wednesday , the only occasion the two peoples join to mark a traditional event .
In a message to mark the Sri Lanka traditional New Year , President Kumaratunga said the New Year is not only a `` golden thread '' that binds together all sections of society but also a festival that portrays unity and coexistence .
She said the New Year festival which is a symbol of unity `` brings to us the message of prosperity along with new hopes and aspirations . ''
`` The foremost hope and aspiration of us all on this day that we visualize the dawn of peace is to build a prosperous Sri Lanka by overcoming all social , economic and political challenges before us , '' said Kumaratunga .
She said there is no other opportunity more suitable than this to promote national unity and strengthen it by specially promoting mutual friendship , goodwill , understanding and respect between the Sinhala and Tamil people .
Sri Lanka has long been plagued by conflicts between the Sinhalese and Tamils , the two largest ethnic groups in the country .
The Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam rebels have been waging a war against the Sri Lankan government for a separate Tamil homeland in the north and east of the country since 1983 .
