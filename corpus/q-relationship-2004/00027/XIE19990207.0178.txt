BUENOS AIRES , February 6 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Argentine sanitary authorities banned the import of English -made chocolates for fear of possible contamination with the `` mad cow '' disease , the news papers here reported Saturday .
The National Health and Quality Service destroyed in the Buenos Aires international airport five kilograms of UK -made chocolate brought by Argentine tourists from Amsterdam .
The authorities said chocolates made in England `` are dangerous because of the `` mad cow '' disease .
`` They can be contaminated with the `` mad cow '' virus which attacks the brain of the animal and can be transmitted to the human brain , '' they said .
