CAIRO , Egypt
-LRB- AP -RRB- --
Libyan leader Moammar Gadhafi presented 11 African leaders with medals of honor in appreciation for violating a U.N. air embargo against Libya , Egypt 's Middle East News Agency reported Thursday .
Four of the African leaders were present at Wednesday night 's ceremony in the northern coastal town of Sirte where Ghadafi presented the medals , MENA said .
They were presidents Ibrahim Bare Mainassara of Niger , Idriss Deby of Chad , Yoweri Museveni of Uganda and Isaias Afwerki of Eritrea , MENA said .
Medals were also received on behalf of presidents Omar el- Bashir of Sudan , Alpha Oumar Konare of Mali , Yaya Jammeh of Gambia , Laurent Kabila of Congo , Robert Mugabe of Zimambwe , Blaise Campaore of Burkina Faso and Ange - Felix Patasse of the Central African Republic .
All the 11 African leaders have in the past three months flown to Libya in violation of U.N. sanctions that ban air travel .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">The Organization of African Unity announced in June that it would no longer abide by the air embargo against Libya</ENAMEX> .
OAU Secretary - General Salim Ahmed Salim also received a medal Wednesday `` for his role in serving the causes of justice and peace in Africa , '' MENA said .
The U.N. Security Council imposed sanctions in 1992 to try to force Gadhafi to surrender two Libyans wanted in the 1988 bombing of a Pan Am jet over Lockerbie , Scotland , that killed 270 people .
