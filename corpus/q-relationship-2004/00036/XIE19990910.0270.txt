WASHINGTON , September 9 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- U.S. President Bill Clinton said he hoped to resume `` constructive conversations '' with China when he meets Chinese President Jiang Zemin this weekend in New Zealand .
Before leaving here for the informal meeting of leaders from the Asian Pacific Economic Cooperation -LRB- APEC -RRB- countries , Clinton told the press that he was eager to restart talks on China 's bid to join the World Trade Organization -LRB- WTO -RRB- .
`` We have done our best to do what I think is the honorable and decent thing in the wake of the terrible accident involving the embassy in Belgrade , '' Clinton said .
The U.S. administration has have made it clear in recent tensions across the Taiwan Straits `` we still support the ` one China ` policy , '' Clinton added .
NATO 's bombing of the Chinese Embassy in Belgrade in May seriously hurt relations between China and the United States and brought WTO negotiations to a standstill .
Clinton said he hoped to `` resume constructive conversations with the Chinese , beginning with my meeting with President Jiang and I would very much like to resume the WTO negotiations . ''
`` I think it would be good for China , good for the United States and good for the world economic system , '' he said .
