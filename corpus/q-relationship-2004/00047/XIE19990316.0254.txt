STOCKHOLM , March 15 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Norwegian Oil and Energy Minister Marit Arnstad said here Monday that his country was ready to further cut its oil output by 100,000 barrels a day in a bid to maintain the stability of oil prices .
Arnstad said after briefing the parliament on the cut plan , that the legislature was supportive of the move .
He said Norway will start to implement the reduction plan on April 1 after the parliament formally endorses it .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">The minister added , however , that the precondition for the Norwegian cut is that members of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries -LRB- OPEC -RRB- curtail their production in accordance with their agreement</ENAMEX> .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">To support OPEC 's efforts to prop up oil prices by production cuts , Norway has cut its daily oil output from the North Sea oil fields by 100,000 barrels since last spring</ENAMEX> .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">Norway 's offshore oil companies have voluntarily reduced oil production under the pressure of falling prices , leading to a larger cut than the government had planned</ENAMEX> .
Statistics showed the companies had actually cut the daily output by 310,000 barrels from 3.4 million barrels to 2.99 million barrels .
Norway , the largest oil producers in west Europe and the second largest oil exporter in the world , yields about 200 million tons of oil and natural gas in oil equivalent annually .
