Sometimes we substitute the term weight for mass, although the two are not identical. Mass is the amount of matter in an object, whereas the weight of an object is how strongly that mass is pulled by gravity. An astronaut in orbit in a space shuttle is weightless, but the astronaut’s mass is the same as it would be on Earth. However, as long as we are earthbound, the weight of an object is a measure of its mass; so we can use the terms interchangeably.
