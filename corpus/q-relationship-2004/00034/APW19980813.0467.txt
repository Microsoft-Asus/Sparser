SEOUL , South Korea
-LRB- AP -RRB- --
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">A South Korean company owned by the Rev. Sun Myung Moon 's Unification Church said Thursday it has signed a contract to send tourists to the scenic east coast of North Korea .</ENAMEX>
This marks the second time the isolated communist state has agreed to tourism deals with businesses in pro- Western South Korea .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">In June , the giant Hyundai conglomerate won North Korean approval to launch a cruise ship tour to Diamond Mountain , a major tourist site on the North 's east coast On Thursday , South Korea 's Tongil Business Group said it also has signed an agreement to send up to 400 tourists a day to the North Korean mountain resort .</ENAMEX>
Pak Po -hi , a top aide to Moon , said the Pyongyang government approved Tongil 's project during his eight-day visit to Pyongyang that ended Tuesday .
Tongil will use high-speed ferries to carry tourists to and from the north on day trips as early as September , Pak said .
Hyundai plans or four-day cruises on luxury liners .
Tongil is a South Korean business arm of the Unification Church .
Its subsidiaries are engaged in weapons making , newspapers , fisheries , real estate , and soft-drink manufacturing .
Both Hyundai and Tongil must also obtain South Korean approval for their North Korean projects .
All business deals between the two Koreas are sensitive to political tensions .
North Korea and South Korea fought a bloody three-year war in the early 1950s that ended in an armistice , not a peace treaty .
South Koreans are required to obtain government approval to visit North Korea or contact North Koreans .
