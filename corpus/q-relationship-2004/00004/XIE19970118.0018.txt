ANKARA , January 17 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Iraqi Patriotic Union of Kurdistan -LRB- PUK -RRB- leader Jalal Talabani said here today his party will cut ties with the Turkish outlawed Kurdish Workers ' Party -LRB- PKK -RRB- .
Talabani said at a press conference : '' We have no confidence in them -LRB- PKK -RRB- , '' adding that there were two PKK camps in northern Iraq , which was under his party 's control .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">The PKK has been fighting a 12-year -old guerrilla war for self-rule in southeastern Turkey</ENAMEX> .
The PUK will not let any Kurdish group to have a military base in northern Iraq and stage attacks against neighboring countries , said Talabani .
`` I also want Turkey to allow the transportation of humanitarian aid of non-governmental organizations to northern Iraq , '' said the PUK leader .
Talabani arrived here Thursday for the exchange of views with Turkish officials on the situation in northern Iraq .
The Iran -backed PUK fought with the Kurdish Democratic Party -LRB- KDP -RRB- , supported by President Saddam Hussein , in late August of last year , which led to U.S. air strike against Iraqi military targets .
The PUK and the KDP then signed a peace deal on October 31 , 1996 under mediation of the United States , Britain and Turkey .
The northern Iraq has been occupied by Kurdish rebels since the Gulf War in 1991 , but it split into rival zones after the KDP and the PUK began fighting each other in 1994 .
