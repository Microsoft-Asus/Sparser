Science, Technology, and Society

CO2 in the atmosphere traps heat and warms the air, just as clear glass does in a greenhouse. Most scientists believe that the CO2 added to the air by the burning of wood and fossil fuels by humans is contributing to a dangerous rise in global temperature. Tropical rain forests are estimated to be responsible for more than 20% of global photosynthesis. It seems reasonable to expect that the lush growth of jungle foliage would reduce global warming by consuming large amounts of CO2, but many experts now believe that rain forests make little or no net contribution to reduction of global warming. Why might this be? (Hint: What happens to the food produced by a rain forest tree when it is eaten by animals or the tree dies?)
