PARIS
-LRB- AP -RRB- -- <ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">Two new cases of so-called mad cow disease have been discovered in northwestern France , local officials said , raising the total number of cases detected in the country this year to 40 .</ENAMEX>
The two animals found to be infected were born in 1994 and 1995 .
French law requires that the animals and the herds they belong to be slaughtered and incinerated .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">French authorities last year discovered 31 cases of mad cow disease , or bovine spongiform encephalopathy .</ENAMEX>
The disease is believed to cause a similar brain-wasting ailment in humans known as Creutzfeld - Jacob disease .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">New cases of the disease are expected to break out in France until 2002 , five years after authorities took rigorous measures to prevent more outbreaks .</ENAMEX>
The disease has an average incubation period of five years .
