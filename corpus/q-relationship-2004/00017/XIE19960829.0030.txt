NEW YORK , August 28 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- The trade between the United States and Central American countries continued its upward climb during the first five months of 1996 .
The Journal of Commerce reported here today that the total U.S. trade with Belize , Costa Rica , El Salvador , Guatemala , Honduras , Nicaragua and Panama rose from 5.57 billion U.S. dollars in the first five months of 1995 to 5.95 billion U.S. dollars in the same period this year .
Analysts expect it will keep growing at a healthy trend as the U.S. forges closer business relationships with its Central American partners .
Total trade last year was 13.73 billion U.S. dollars , up 15 percent from 11.92 billion U.S. dollars in 1994 , with goods moving between the United States and Nicaragua jumping the most , 38 percent , followed by U.S. trade with Honduras , up 29 percent .
The paper also reported that Central America 's trade volume with the United States will surpass U.S. - Caribbean trade within two to three years .
Last year , U.S. imports from Central America rose 20 percent , double the growth rate of U.S. imports from the entire Latin American region , according to the paper .
In the first five months of 1996 , U.S. imports , mainly woven and knit apparel , fruits and nuts , spices , coffee and tea , reached 2.9 billion U.S. dollars , up from 2.5 billion U.S. dollars a year earlier .
