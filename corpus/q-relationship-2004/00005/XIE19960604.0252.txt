COLOMBO , June 4 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- The Indian authorities have recovered 17,000 rounds of ammunition in a Sri Lankan Tamil rebel 's hideout on the southern Indian coast , according to reports reaching here today .
The ammunition was mainly 7.62 round for T -56 rifles and Russian AK 47s .
According to defense sources , the Indian security authorities are remaining on alert to combat Tamil rebels using Southern India as a transit point for armaments coming to Sri Lanka , the reports said .
India banned the rebels of the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam -LRB- LTTE -RRB- on May 4 , 1992 after they assassinated former Indian Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi .
<ENAMEX TYPE="OKAY">They have also sought the extradition of LTTE 's head Velupillai Prabhakaran , intelligence chief Pottu Amman and head of the women 's brigade Akila .</ENAMEX>
Akila , who had trained the suicide bomber who killed Rajiv Gandhi , was reported to have been killed in the Sri Lankan government 's major military operation in Jaffna Peninsula last November .
According to local press reports , the LTTE fighting for a separate Tamil state in the north and east of Sri Lanka is facing a serious shortage of ammunition .
