COLOMBO , June 9 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- The Sri Lankan government of People ` s Alliance Friday continued peace talks with the main opposition United National Party -LRB- UNP -RRB- as fierce fighting raged between government troops and Tamil Tiger rebels in the northern Jaffna peninsula .
Two delegations , led respectively by President Kumaratunga and UNP leader Wickremesinghe , had discussed the key land issue in the radical power-sharing constitutional reforms , aimed at ending the 17-year ethnic conflicts between majority Sinhalese and minority Tamils .
`` The two delegations continued the discussions on provisions relating to land in the proposed constitution , agreement was reached on several matters , '' said a joint communique issued by the two parties .
It said the subcommittee on land will consider the remaining issues and report to the plenary .
During the talks President Kumaratunga emphasized the need to reach agreement on all matters relating to the proposed constitution expeditiously , said the communique .
Kumaratunga had said she was keen to conclude talks with the UNP by mid- June and press ahead with the constitutional reforms at the earliest .
Analysts here believed that a bipartisan approach between the government and the UNP was crucial for any peace process to succeed , as together they represent almost the entire majority Sinhalese community .
Any agreement between the two main parties must be presented to parliament and approved before it stands automatically dissolved on August 24 .
Thereafter , fresh elections must be held by November .
Meanwhile , fierce fighting erupted in Jaffna on Friday with Tamil Tiger rebels launching a dawn attack on military defenses around the town of Koddikamam but troops had beaten back the rebel assault .
The rebels of Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam -LRB- LTTE -RRB- have been fighting for a separate state for Tamils in the country 's north and east since 1983 .
The LTTE rebels , who were accused of assassinating Minister of Industrial Development C. V. Gunaratne on Wednesday , have opposed the government 's constitutional reforms .
However , Norway has been trying to bring the LTTE and the Sri Lankan government to the negotiating table even as both sides remained locked in fierce combat in Jaffna .
