ANKARA , June 19 -LRB- Xinhua -RRB- -- Turkey has started to withdraw some parts of its troops from northern Iraq , the semi-official Anatolia News Agency reported tonight .
The agency said that Turkey began to retreat some of its security forces from the areas under the control of Iraq 's Kurdistan Democratic Party -LRB- KDP -RRB- led by Mesud Barzani in the region .
More than 50 tanks and armored vehicles returned to Turkey from northern Iraq over the past week after completing their mission , the agency said , adding that the military operation is continuing along the Iranian border in pursuit of the rebels of the outlawed Kurdish Workers ' Party -LRB- PKK -RRB- .
<ENAMEX TYPE="VITAL">Turkey has repeatedly accused Iran of supporting the PKK , which has been fighting a separatist war for autonomy in eastern and southeastern Turkey since August 1984</ENAMEX> .
Iranian President Hashemi Rafsanjani said Sunday in Istanbul , the largest city of Turkey , that there were no rebels of the PKK guerrillas on the Iranian territory .
Six government security forces were killed and five others wounded late Wednesday night by PKK members in armed attacks in Gorenoluk village in Eruh town of southeastern province of Siirt on their way back from the cross-border military operation .
